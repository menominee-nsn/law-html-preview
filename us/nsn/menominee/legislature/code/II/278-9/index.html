Code of the Menominee Indian Tribe

§ 278-9 Guardian ad litem and other appointed persons.

A. The Court shall establish by rule the appointment guidelines, additional qualifications, and compensation for guardians ad litem ("GAL"), defense counsel, special advocates or other appointed persons. [Amended 5-20-2021 by Ord. No. 21-42]

B. The Court may appoint defense counsel, guardians ad litem or other counsel at its own discretion when it determines it is in the best interest of the child.

C. Guardian ad litem. [Added 5-20-2021 by Ord. No. 21-42]

(1) A guardian ad litem may be certified by the Court if he or she has completed guardian ad litem training or training requirements provided by the Court.

(2) The court may recognize a guardian ad litem as certified if he or she has completed guardian ad litem training provided by another Indian tribe, or a state, or is recognized as a certified guardian ad litem by another jurisdiction.

(3) Guardian ad litem duties and responsibilities:

(a) The guardian ad litem shall be an advocate for the best interests of the person or unborn child for whom the appointment is made.

(b) The guardian ad litem shall function independently, in the same manner as an attorney for a party to the action, and shall consider, but shall not be bound by, the wishes of that person or the positions of others as to the best interests of that person or unborn child.

(c) If the guardian ad litem determines that the best interests of the person are substantially inconsistent with wishes of that person, the guardian ad litem shall so inform the Court and the Court may appoint counsel to represent that person. The guardian ad litem has not the rights or duties of a general guardian.

(4) In any matter involving a child found to be in need of protection and services, the guardian ad litem may, if reappointed or if the appointment is continued, do any of the following:

(a) Participate in permanency planning.

(b) Petition for change in placement.

(c) Petition for termination of parental rights.

(d) Petition for modification or extension of dispositional order.

(e) Petition for a temporary restraining order or injunction.

(f) Petition for relief from a judgment terminating parental rights.

(g) Petition for an appointment of guardian, revision of guardianship order, or removal of guardian.

(h) Bring an action or motion for the determination of the child's paternity.

(i) Perform any other duties consistent with this chapter.

(5) In any matter involving a contested suspension or termination of parental rights, a guardian ad litem shall:

(a) Provide information to the Court relating to the parent's competency to participate in the proceeding.

(b) Provide assistance to the Court and the parent's adversary counsel in protecting the parent's right in the proceeding.

(c) The guardian ad litem may not participate in the proceeding as a party, and may not call witnesses, provide opening statements or closing arguments, or participate in any activity at trial that is required to be performed by the parent's adversary counsel.

(6) Termination and extension of appointment.

(a) The appointment terminates upon the entry of the Court's final order or upon the termination of any appeal in which the guardian ad litem participates;

(b) The guardian ad litem may appeal, may participate in an appeal or may do neither. If an appeal is taken by any party and the guardian ad litem chooses not to participate in that appeal, he or she shall file with the appellate court a statement of reasons for not participating.

[1] Irrespective of the guardian ad litem's decision not to participate in an appeal, the appellate court may order the guardian ad litem to participate in the appeal.

(7) At any time, the guardian ad litem, any party or the person from whom the appointment is made may request in writing or on the record that the court extend or terminate the appointment or reappointment.

(8) The Court may extend that appointment, or reappoint a guardian ad litem appointed under this section, after the entry of the final order or after the termination of the appeal, but the court shall specifically state the scope of the responsibilities of the guardian ad litem during the period of that extension or reappointment.

(9) Primary duties include:

(a) Investigations, home visits, monitoring and making reports to the Tribal Court.

(b) Submit written reports and may testify, make findings and recommendations as to the best course of action to be taken in the interest of children/Indian children.

(c) Expected to work closely with families in securing their trust that the tribal justice system will be fair in determining the child's best interest.

(d) Close collaboration with service providers and agencies and appreciation of tribal culture and traditions are required.

(e) Open and organize files.

(f) Investigate facts by conducting interview(s), home visits, and reviewing a prior court action/reports.

(g) Submit professional written reports with recommendations to Tribal Court prior to Court hearings.

(h) Give in-court testimony concerning findings and recommendations.

(i) Monitor progress of child and family after court issues disposition orders.

(j) Maintain contact with child and family until case is closed.

(k) Bring significant changes to the Court's attention.

(l) Provide a written report to the Court regarding the best interests of the child, including conclusions and recommendations and the facts upon which they are based.

(10) Guardian ad litem training.

(a) The Court shall set and monitor the guardian ad litem training. Training required under Wisconsin Supreme Court Rule 35.01 shall be recognized.

(b) Requirements to meet with and interview the child(ren) shall not only be a brief meeting right before court.

(c) Talking directly to parents, foster parents, and other individuals involved in the case to obtain an independent opinion about what is in the child's best interest should be based solely on conversations with caseworker or the caseworker's written report.

D. The Court may appoint a special advocate under a Court-approved special advocate program to promote the best interest of the child. Such an advocate does not become party to the proceedings and may not make motions or call or cross-examine witnesses. Any designation of special advocate terminates when the jurisdiction of the Court over the child terminates unless the special advocate is discharged by Court order sooner.

E. If parent(s) are not already represented, the Court shall appoint counsel to parent(s) of a child who will be permanently removed from the parent(s) home, when the Court becomes aware that the child will be permanently removed from the parent(s) home in termination of parental rights, suspension of parental rights and permanent guardianship proceedings.

F. The Court shall appoint counsel to a child in all matters where the child is the petitioning party.
