Code of the Menominee Indian Tribe

§ 290-70 Violations and penalties.

Whoever intentionally conveys or causes to be conveyed any threat or false information, knowing such to be false, concerning an attempt or alleged attempt being made or to be made to destroy any property by the means of explosives is guilty of a tribal crime and subject to the following penalties:

A. Penalties. Upon conviction, the Court shall impose a fine of not less than $1,000 nor more than $5,000 and a jail term of not less than six months nor more than one year in tribal jail. In addition to the above penalties, the Court may order the defendant to pay for all expenses caused as a result of the threat, including but not limited to evacuation costs, operational costs and property damage. In the case of a violation of this article by a student pursuant to § 290-71 below, the Court shall have broad discretion pursuant to § 290-71B(2) to determine the penalties and/or the disposition; provided, however, that in no case shall the Court assess a penalty in excess of $5,000 and/or a jail term exceeding six months, if the student is an adult, or to determine disposition if the student is prosecuted under the tribally adopted juvenile code.10
