Code of the Menominee Indian Tribe

§ 550-36 Department authority and responsibilities.

A. The Department shall be responsible for the administration of this article and is delegated the authority to create rules to allow for the implementation of this article.

B. Fees. The Menominee Tribal Legislature, upon recommendation of the Department, shall adopt a motor vehicle registration fee schedule. Upon adoption, the fee schedule shall be published in the Menominee Nation News and shall be republished whenever fees are changed. All registration fees shall be nonrefundable and shall be paid to and retained by the Department. The Department may recommend separate fees based on any combination of the following:

(1) The type of motor vehicle being registered.

(2) The maximum gross weight of the motor vehicle. Upon payment of the fee, a motor vehicle subject to registration on the basis of gross weight may be registered at a weight in excess of the manufacturer's maximum gross weight rating, but such registration does not exempt such vehicle from compliance with weigh limitations imposed by law or by state, local, or tribal authorities pursuant to authority of law.

(3) Whether the motor vehicle's registration was previously suspended under this article.

C. Records retention. All registration applications and renewal requests shall be retained by the Department.

D. Registration lists. The Department shall maintain a list of registrations. The list shall give the name and address of each registrant, the registration number assigned, and other identifying information, as the Department deems necessary.

(1) Upon request, the Department shall distribute, free of charge, registration lists compiled under this section to the following:

(a) The Menominee Tribal Police Department.

(b) Any other person, agency, or public officer that provides, in writing, a reasonable request for the lists.

(2) Any person who has received a personal identifier of any person under § 550-36D(1) shall keep the personal identifier confidential and may not disclose it except:

(a) To perform a legally authorized function;

(b) To issue or renew a property and/or casualty insurance policy, related underwriting, billing, processing or paying a claim;

(c) To conduct a vehicle recall by the manufacturer of a vehicle or his or her agent.

(3) Any person who discloses a personal identifier in violation of this section may be subject to a fine of not more than $500 for each violation.

(4) Any person who requests or obtains a personal identifier from the Department under false pretenses may be required to forfeit not more than $500 for each violation.
