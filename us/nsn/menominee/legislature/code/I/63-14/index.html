Code of the Menominee Indian Tribe

§ 63-14 Tallying of regular and absentee ballots.

A. Official tallying location. All ballots cast at the polls and cast absentee shall be tallied at the polling location that is the last to close pursuant to § 63-17 of this chapter.

B. Tallying of ballots cast at the election polls.

(1) The Commission shall begin tallying ballots cast at the election polls immediately following the closing of the polls on the last day of the election.

(2) All ballots cast at the polls shall be counted, except:

(a) Ballots containing votes that exceed the number of votes able to be cast in the election; or

(b) Ballots containing more than the number of votes able to be cast for an off-Reservation candidate; or

(c) Ballots that do not bear the endorsement of a Commission official or agent.

(3) The Commission shall record:

(a) The number of ballots that were cast at each polling location;

(b) The number of ballots that were cast and counted;

(c) The number of ballots that were cast but not counted, including a brief description as to why such ballots were not counted; and

(d) Such other information as the Commission may determine.

(4) The Commission shall tally the votes in a private setting free of distractions by the public, but election poll monitors shall be allowed to be present during the tally.

(5) The tally, tie breaker and certification of results shall, to the maximum extent practical, be broadcast via webcast or utilizing some other multimedia means.

C. Tallying of absentee ballots cast.

(1) The Commission shall begin tallying absentee ballots cast immediately following the tallying of ballots cast at the polls.

(2) All absentee ballots cast shall be counted, except:

(a) Ballots containing votes that exceed the number of votes able to be cast in the election; or

(b) Ballots containing more than the number of votes able to be cast for an off-Reservation candidate; or

(c) Ballots that have been superseded by a specially marked ballot; or

(d) Ballots that do not bear the endorsement of a Commission official or agent; or

(e) Ballots that were contained in an envelope along with other absentee ballots; or

(f) Ballots contained in the absentee ballot box designated for nonconforming ballots.

(3) The Commission shall record:

(a) The number of ballots that were cast at each polling location;

(b) The number of ballots that were cast and counted;

(c) The number of ballots that were cast but not counted, including a brief description as to why such ballots were not counted; and

(d) Such other information as the Commission may determine.

(4) The Commission shall tally the absentee votes in a private setting free of distractions by the public, but election poll monitors shall be allowed to be present during the tally.

(5) The tally, tie breaker and certification of results shall, to the maximum extent practical, be broadcast via webcast or utilizing some other multimedia means.
