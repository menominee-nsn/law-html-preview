Code of the Menominee Indian Tribe

§ 625-150 Traffic visibility.

A. At every intersection of two public roads or a public road and a railroad right-of-way there shall be a traffic visibility triangle. Within the triangle, no obstructions such as structures, parking or vegetation shall be allowed between 2.5 feet and 10 feet above the elevation of the roadway.

B. Such traffic visibility triangles shall be formed by the intersecting center lines and a line connecting points on the center lines of the intersecting highways or railroad right-of-way at the following distances:

(1) On Class A and B highways, 300 feet from the intersecting center lines.

(2) On Class C and D highways, 100 feet from the intersecting center lines.

(3) On railway right-of-way, 200 feet from the center of the highway along the center of the railroad right-of-way.
